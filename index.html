The Eagle

An eagle's eyesight is so good that an eagle can apparently see a mouse on the ground when the eagle is still high in the sky.

  • Scientific Name: Aquila chrysaetos
  • Average Length: 66cm to 102cm
  • Average Lifespan: Estimated 39 years
  • Habitat: High Mountains

The golden eagle (Aquila chrysaetos) is one of the best-known birds of prey in the Northern Hemisphere. Golden eagles maintain home ranges or territories that may be as large as 200 km2 (77 sq mi). They build large nests in cliffs and other high places to which they may return for several breeding years.
